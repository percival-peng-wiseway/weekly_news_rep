Albanese Government to Legislate: Large Data Centres Must Become "Net Generators"
Energy-Storage.news · 2026-07-19 · Source
PM Albanese announced at the University of Sydney on Jul 15 that next-generation large-scale data centres will be legally required to build new renewable generation and storage, injecting no less than their consumption into the grid and bearing full connection costs. A new AI Office will be established within the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet. The government aims to reach consensus with states at next month's National Cabinet and introduce legislation early next year. (Also covered by ESD News Jul 19: Swinburne professor notes well-sited data centres could unlock stranded renewable projects; AEMO-commissioned study estimates data centres may account for ~6% of NEM consumption by 2030.)

AEMO Connections Scorecard: Record 9.1 GW Added in FY26, Batteries Dominate but Wind/Solar Still Lagging
RenewEconomy · 2026-07-19 · Source
AEMO's latest Connections Scorecard reveals 9.1 GW of new capacity reached full output in FY26 (to Jun 30) — more than double the prior year. However, over 5 GW was battery storage, with new wind and solar falling short of 4 GW, well below the pace needed for the 2030 82% renewable target. Wind farm applications hit a record 12 projects totalling 5.2 GW. The overall connection pipeline grew 42% to 75.4 GW, though registrations and application approvals (7.4 GW / 14.2 GW) were down year-on-year.
